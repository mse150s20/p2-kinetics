2/29/2020
Date the United States first reports a coronavirus death and announces travel restrictions. (10)

3/11/2020
The World Health Organization officially names the disease and classifies COVID-19 as a pandemic. (10)

3/13/2020
Date that testing confirmed the first case of COVID-19 in the state of Idaho. (7)

3/13/2020
Date that the decision was made to deliver all BSU classes remotely, which began the following Monday. (7)

3/25/2020
Date that Governor Brad Little issued a statewide stay-at-home order till Apri 30, 2020. (7)
First confirmed case of COVID-19 coincides with the BSU announcement while the self-isolation notice occurred 12 days later.

3/26/2020
The United States leads the world in confirmed coronavirus cases. (10)

4/17/2020
Date that more than 1,000 Boise conservative groups plan to protest stay-at-home order. (9)

4/23/2020
Idaho Gov. Brad Little has laid out his four step plan for reopening the state's economy. (5)

4/25/2020
Global death toll from COVID-19 surpasses 200,000. Idaho's COVID-19 related death toll is now 56 people and 1,760 confirmed cases. (11)

4/28/2020
Cases in Idaho reached 1,984. Talks of reopening continue.

4/29/2020
Idaho cases increased to 1,831 reported cases.

4/30/2020
Idaho Cases rose to 1,860 infected.

5/1/2020
Number of Idaho cases rose to 1,880 showing a decrease of daily transmissions. Phase 1 of Re-Opening begins.
